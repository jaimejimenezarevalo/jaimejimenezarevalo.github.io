About

Mathematics as curiosity, resilience, and community

I am a first-generation undergraduate mathematics student and researcher at California State University, Dominguez Hills. My path into mathematics began with the puzzle-like satisfaction of solving problems and grew through math and science competitions, college coursework, tutoring, and research.

After transferring from Long Beach City College, I began conducting research in coding theory and combinatorics. My long-term goal is to earn a doctoral degree, contribute to mathematical research, and expand access to academic opportunities for students from underrepresented communities.

Current interests

Undergraduate research through Combinatorics and Coding Theory in the Tropics at the University of Puerto Rico in Ponce. I study the mathematical structure of affine Grassmann codes and develop proof-based and computational approaches to the project.

Liar's Bingo and Error-Correcting Codes

Research at CSUDH examining Liar's Bingo through the lens of coding theory. I use Python simulations to model strip constructions, introduce controlled errors, and investigate generalizations to different bases and strip sizes.

Symmetric Matrices over $\mathbb{F}_3$

A developing investigation of symmetric matrices over finite fields, including enumeration, rank classification, and partitions of field elements. The project uses code to expose patterns that can guide proof and further research.

$$M=\begin{pmatrix}a & b\\ b & c\end{pmatrix},\qquad a,b,c\in\mathbb{F}_3$$
